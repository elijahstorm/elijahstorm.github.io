Chess AI (Solo)

A relatively simple AI test project I made just to experiment with AI. As a long time avid chess player, I've always wanted to break down and figure out how Chess Engines worked. I am currently creating this as a side project, and I expect to be done by May 24th - 31th.

Using iterative deepening and advanced pruning techniques, the AI engine currently picks what it thinks is the best move, while looking 6 moves ahead in 100ms (suprisingly impressive speeds); however, my evaluate function is not as accurate as I would like. This chess AI by no means boasts being the best in the world, and will most likely fall to an engine like Stockfish.

I recently redeigned the interface, and used multi-threaded, delayed computation to provide a smoother user experience and allow a beautifully integrated Evaluation bar that will act and adjust even on the Players turn. Before, the CPU intensive search function would lock the page until it finished searching the millions of lines that it might need to get a clear idea of the position before moving. Again, I was suprised by how well the results turned out, because I was worried it would delay the Search function by so much that it would be agonizingly slow. However, there was little to no noticable slowdown issue from having the Multi-Threaded Delay Search approach, to not having it.

My next current goal is to evaulate down unstable capturing lines so that the horizion effect of heuristic AI does not affect my engine as much. My biggest roadblock is fine-tuning the heatmaps and tactic evaulations. A good chess player will have a good understanding of tactics, and my AI just brute forces evaulations, and therefore misses tactics and processes them slowly.

I usually work this in my free time, so it's a bit slow to finish :)

Game - ThunderLite (Solo)

This is a live, online, multiplayer game that I created back in 2018 based off of a slightly popular online game from the late 2000s that I played. I was always fascinated how the game worked and I wanted to recreate it, but never got around to it until after High School. The original game has long since been abandoned by its creators, and the community fell apart. I thought it would be a fun and harmless project to recreate something I once found so much joy in!

Coded entirely in HTML5, JavaScript and Node.js, I didn't stop at making the game and it's Engine. I decided to make a user database and include user profiles, with custom information and details! Also a fun level-up system. I used MongoDB and Socket.io to make a live playable game that was cross-platform for mobile and desktop users. I also created an AI that actively found great moves and kept the offline play interesting as well!
